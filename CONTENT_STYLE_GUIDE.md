# Thalen Technologies Content Style Guide

**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** December 2024  
**Purpose:** Maintain consistent, professional, and factual tone across all website content

---

## Executive Summary

This style guide establishes content standards for Thalen Technologies' website and marketing materials. The guide emphasizes factual, service-oriented communication appropriate for government technology consulting. All content should demonstrate expertise through specific credentials, verifiable metrics, and concrete implementation details rather than promotional language or unsubstantiated claims.

---

## Core Principles

### Professional Government Technology Tone

Thalen Technologies serves government agencies at federal, state, and local levels. Our content must reflect the professional standards expected in government contracting environments. This means prioritizing clarity, accuracy, and verifiable information over marketing hyperbole.

**Guiding Philosophy:** Let our work speak for itself through specific examples, measurable outcomes, and documented credentials rather than self-promotional language.

### Factual Over Promotional

Every claim should be supported by specific evidence. When describing capabilities, reference actual implementations, certifications, or documented experience. Avoid subjective assessments of quality that cannot be verified.

### Service-Oriented Language

Frame content around client needs and outcomes rather than company achievements. Focus on what we deliver and how it benefits agencies rather than how impressive our services are.

## Language Guidelines

### Words and Phrases to Avoid

The following terms create promotional tone inappropriate for government technology consulting:

| Avoid | Why | Use Instead |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| "Industry-leading" | Unverifiable claim | "Experienced in [specific area]" |
| "Best-in-class" | Subjective assessment | "Certified in [specific standard]" |
| "Cutting-edge" | Marketing jargon | "Current [technology name] implementation" |
| "Revolutionary" | Hyperbolic | "Modernized approach to [process]" |
| "Game-changing" | Promotional | "Significant improvement in [metric]" |
| "Transformative" | Vague superlative | "Achieved [X%] improvement in [metric]" |
| "Proven track record" | Generic claim | "Completed [X] projects for [agencies]" |
| "Unparalleled expertise" | Unverifiable | "15+ years experience in [domain]" |
| "World-class" | Subjective | "Holds [specific certifications]" |
| "State-of-the-art" | Marketing term | "[Technology name] platform" |
| "Seamless integration" | Overused buzzword | "Integrated with [X] systems" |
| "Turnkey solution" | Vague promise | "Implementation includes [specific deliverables]" |
| "Mission-critical" | Overused in govtech | "Supports [specific mission function]" (use sparingly) |

### Acceptable Alternatives

Replace promotional language with specific, verifiable statements:

**Instead of:** "Our industry-leading automation solutions deliver transformative results"  
**Write:** "We implement FedRAMP-authorized automation platforms including UiPath Gov, Automation Anywhere Gov, and Blue Prism Gov"

**Instead of:** "Proven track record of success across government agencies"  
**Write:** "Completed 47 automation implementations for federal agencies from 2018-2024"

**Instead of:** "Best-in-class cybersecurity expertise"  
**Write:** "Team holds CISSP, CEH, and CMMC Level 3 certifications with active TS/SCI clearances"

## Content Structure Standards

### Headlines and Subheadings

Headlines should be descriptive and service-focused rather than promotional.

**Avoid:**
- "Transform Your Agency with Our Revolutionary Solutions"
- "The Future of Government Technology is Here"
- "Unmatched Expertise You Can Trust"

**Prefer:**
- "Government Process Automation Implementation Services"
- "FedRAMP Platform Integration and Deployment"
- "Cybersecurity Assessment and Compliance Support"

### Service Descriptions

Service descriptions must include specific platforms, methodologies, or deliverables rather than generic benefits.

**Template Structure:**
1. **What we do:** Specific service offering
2. **How we do it:** Platforms, methodologies, or frameworks used
3. **What you get:** Concrete deliverables or outcomes

**Example:**

We implement FedRAMP-authorized robotic process automation platforms for government agencies. Our services include process assessment, platform selection from UiPath Gov, Automation Anywhere Gov, or Blue Prism Gov, bot development, ATO documentation support, and Center of Excellence establishment. Typical implementations automate 8-15 processes within the first six months.

### Case Study and Results Language

When describing project outcomes, use specific metrics with context rather than percentages alone.

**Weak:** "Achieved 85% efficiency improvement"  
**Strong:** "Reduced claims processing time from 45 minutes to 6 minutes per claim, processing 180,000 claims in the first year"

**Weak:** "Delivered significant cost savings"  
**Strong:** "Eliminated 85,000 hours of manual data processing annually, resulting in $12M cost reduction"

**Weak:** "Dramatically improved security posture"  
**Strong:** "Implemented zero-trust architecture across 14 systems, achieving CMMC Level 3 compliance within 8 months"

## Metrics and Data Presentation

### Quantitative Claims

All quantitative claims must be specific and contextual.

**Required Elements:**
- Baseline measurement
- Final measurement
- Timeframe
- Scope (number of systems, users, transactions, etc.)

**Example:**

"Reduced average benefits processing time from 28 days to 11 days over 12-month implementation. System processes 50,000 applications monthly with 99.2% accuracy rate."

### Qualitative Outcomes

When metrics are not available, describe outcomes in concrete operational terms.

**Avoid:** "Greatly improved user satisfaction"  
**Prefer:** "Implemented self-service portal reducing help desk tickets by 3,200 per month"

**Avoid:** "Enhanced security significantly"  
**Prefer:** "Deployed SIEM platform monitoring 14 systems with automated threat detection and 24/7 SOC coverage"

## Team and Credential Presentation

### Leadership Biographies

Team bios should emphasize verifiable credentials and specific experience rather than subjective assessments.

**Required Elements:**
- Specific previous employers or roles
- Years of experience in relevant domains
- Named certifications with issuing organizations
- Security clearance levels (if applicable)
- Concrete accomplishments (projects led, systems implemented, etc.)

**Avoid:**
- "Expert in cybersecurity with deep knowledge"
- "Seasoned professional with extensive background"
- "Recognized leader in the industry"

**Prefer:**
- "Served as cybersecurity analyst at NSA from 2010-2018"
- "Holds CISSP and CEH certifications with TS/SCI clearance"
- "Led FedRAMP authorization processes for six government cloud migrations"

**Example Bio:**

Managed cloud infrastructure migrations for defense contractors requiring Impact Level 5 compliance. AWS Certified DevOps Professional with experience in Kubernetes orchestration. Previously led infrastructure teams at Northrop Grumman and SAIC. Holds active Secret clearance and has supported 12 ATO processes.

### Certification and Compliance Language

When referencing certifications, use full official names on first mention.

**Format:** "[Certification Full Name] ([Common Abbreviation])"

**Examples:**
- Federal Risk and Authorization Management Program (FedRAMP)
- Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification (CMMC)
- Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP)
- Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH)

## Call-to-Action Language

### Button and Link Text

CTAs should be neutral and descriptive rather than urgent or promotional.

**Avoid:**
- "Schedule Your Strategic Assessment Today!"
- "Transform Your Operations Now"
- "Unlock Your Agency's Potential"
- "Don't Miss This Opportunity"

**Prefer:**
- "Request Assessment"
- "Contact Us"
- "Schedule Consultation"
- "View Case Studies"
- "Download Resources"

### Form Submission Language

Confirmation messages should be professional and set clear expectations.

**Avoid:** "Thank you! We're excited to transform your operations!"  
**Prefer:** "Thank you for your inquiry. A team member will contact you within two business days."

## Platform and Technology References

### Naming Conventions

Always use official product names with proper capitalization and government-specific variants where applicable.

**Commercial vs. Government Versions:**
- UiPath → UiPath Government Cloud (for gov implementations)
- Microsoft Power BI → Microsoft Power BI Gov
- AWS → AWS GovCloud (when discussing government deployments)
- Azure → Azure Government

### FedRAMP and Compliance References

When referencing FedRAMP authorization, include impact level where relevant.

**Examples:**
- "FedRAMP Moderate authorization"
- "FedRAMP High impact level"
- "StateRAMP authorized for state and local government"

## Content Review Checklist

Before publishing any content, verify the following:

### Tone and Language
- [ ] No superlatives ("best," "leading," "revolutionary")
- [ ] No unverifiable claims ("proven," "unparalleled")
- [ ] No marketing jargon ("cutting-edge," "game-changing")
- [ ] Service-focused rather than self-promotional
- [ ] Professional government contracting tone

### Specificity
- [ ] Platforms named specifically (not "leading tools")
- [ ] Metrics include baseline, outcome, timeframe, and scope
- [ ] Certifications referenced by full official name
- [ ] Experience quantified (years, number of projects, agencies served)
- [ ] Outcomes described with operational context

### Credentials
- [ ] Team bios include specific employers and roles
- [ ] Certifications listed with issuing organizations
- [ ] Security clearances specified where applicable
- [ ] Technical skills referenced by specific technologies

### Accuracy
- [ ] All metrics are verifiable from project records
- [ ] Certification names match official designations
- [ ] Platform names use correct government variants
- [ ] Compliance references include appropriate levels/frameworks

## Revision Examples

### Homepage Hero Section

**Before:**
"Transform Your Agency with Industry-Leading Automation Solutions That Deliver Revolutionary Results"

**After:**
"Government Technology Implementation Services"

"We deliver end-to-end technology solutions that modernize government operations, enhance security, and improve citizen services through proven implementation expertise."

### Service Page Introduction

**Before:**
"Our world-class team of automation experts brings unparalleled expertise and cutting-edge solutions to deliver transformative results for government agencies nationwide."

**After:**
"We implement FedRAMP and StateRAMP-authorized automation platforms for federal, state, and local government agencies. Our team holds active security clearances and maintains certifications in UiPath, Automation Anywhere, Blue Prism, and ServiceNow platforms."

### Case Study Description

**Before:**
"We delivered a game-changing automation solution that revolutionized the agency's operations and achieved incredible results that exceeded all expectations."

**After:**
"We implemented UiPath Government Cloud to automate 12 claims processing workflows. The implementation reduced manual processing time from 45 minutes to 6 minutes per claim and processed 180,000 claims in the first year with 99.1% accuracy."

### Team Biography

**Before:**
"John is a seasoned cybersecurity expert with deep knowledge and extensive experience delivering best-in-class security solutions for mission-critical government systems."

**After:**
"John served as cybersecurity analyst at NSA from 2010-2018. He designed security architectures for automated intelligence systems and holds CISSP and CEH certifications. He currently leads security assessments for government automation projects and maintains TS/SCI clearance."
