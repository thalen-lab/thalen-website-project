# Incentro Data Solutions Analysis & NexDyne Service Mapping

## Incentro's 6 Data Subpages Summary

### 1. Data Strategy
**Focus:** Strategic planning and roadmap development
**Key Elements:**
- Data Maturity Model assessment
- 4-8 week "Data Strategy Basecamp"
- Stakeholder engagement and buy-in
- Roadmap with quick wins and long-term goals
- 5 aspects: data maturity, available data, processes, people/culture, technology

**NexDyne Mapping:** → **Data Analytics & Intelligence** (new service page)

---

### 2. Data Platform
**Focus:** Technical infrastructure and data architecture
**Key Elements:**
- Single Source of Truth (data warehouse, data lake, data mesh)
- Scalable cloud-based infrastructure
- Data migration and integration
- Automated data pipelines
- Data quality, governance, and security
- Tech-agnostic approach

**NexDyne Mapping:** → **Cloud Infrastructure & Modernization** (existing service)
**Rationale:** This is fundamentally cloud infrastructure work - building scalable data platforms on AWS/Azure/GCP. Fits perfectly under Cloud Modernization as a specialized offering.

---

### 3. Data Visualization
**Focus:** Analytics dashboards and business intelligence
**Key Elements:**
- Custom dashboard development
- Real-time insights and KPIs
- Process-first approach (not technology-first)
- Integration with GenAI for report generation
- Actionable insights for decision-making

**NexDyne Mapping:** → **Data Analytics & Intelligence** (new service page)

---

### 4. Data Training
**Focus:** Upskilling teams on data tools and practices
**Key Elements:**
- Hands-on training (not theory)
- All skill levels (newbie to expert)
- Custom company-specific training
- Training on the job during projects
- Tools: Python, Java, Pentaho ETL, etc.
- Goal: Make organizations self-reliant

**NexDyne Mapping:** → **Digital Transformation Enablement** (existing service)
**Rationale:** This is change management and capability building, which is core to Digital Transformation. Could also be a sub-offering under Data Analytics if we want to keep all data services together.

---

### 5. Content Service Platform (CSP)
**Focus:** Document/content management and governance
**Key Elements:**
- Manage unstructured data (documents, contracts, emails, audio, video)
- Federated model (no costly migrations)
- Version control and audit trails
- Compliance and risk management
- Alfresco platform implementation
- Role-based permissions

**NexDyne Mapping:** → **Application Development & Integration** OR **Cybersecurity & Compliance**
**Rationale:** This is NOT really a data analytics service - it's enterprise content management (ECM). Could fit under:
- Application Development (as a specialized platform implementation)
- Cybersecurity & Compliance (emphasis on governance, audit trails, compliance)
**Recommendation:** Skip this for now - it's not core to NexDyne's positioning and would require specialized Alfresco expertise.

---

### 6. Data Engineering
**Focus:** Staff augmentation and technical implementation
**Key Elements:**
- Data pipeline development
- ETL processes
- Data quality management
- Cloud platform expertise
- Team augmentation / consulting
- Knowledge transfer

**NexDyne Mapping:** → **Data Analytics & Intelligence** (new service page) OR as a cross-cutting capability
**Rationale:** Data Engineering is the "how" that enables all data initiatives. Could be positioned as:
- A subpage under Data Analytics & Intelligence
- A cross-cutting capability mentioned across multiple services
**Recommendation:** Make it a subpage under Data Analytics & Intelligence, but also reference it in Cloud Infrastructure (for data platform engineering).

---

- Budget forecasting and financial analytics
