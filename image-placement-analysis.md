# Image Placement Analysis for Hero Sections

## General Principles

### Image on the Left (Recommended for Most Cases)

**Advantages:**
- **Natural reading flow**: In left-to-right languages (like English), users scan from left to right. Starting with a visual creates immediate engagement before they process the text
- **Visual hierarchy**: The image acts as an anchor point that draws the eye first, then guides it naturally to the headline and CTA
- **Proven pattern**: Most high-converting landing pages (Stripe, Linear, Vercel) use left-aligned visuals for their hero sections

### Image on the Right

**Works well when:**
- You want to emphasize the text/headline first (text-heavy products, technical documentation)
- Creating visual variety in alternating sections (left-right-left pattern down the page)
- The image is supplementary rather than the main focal point

## NexDyne-Specific Recommendation

**Recommended: Image on the Left**

**Rationale:**
1. You're selling a tech/AI product where the visual (dashboard, interface, or abstract tech visual) creates immediate credibility
2. The visual impact helps establish the "Intelligence, Delivered" brand promise before users read the details
3. It creates a strong first impression that balances professionalism with visual interest

## Implementation Options

- **Image-left layout**: Creates immediate visual engagement, then guides to text and CTA
- **Image-right layout**: Text-forward approach, emphasizes messaging before visuals
